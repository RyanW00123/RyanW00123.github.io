    Did You Know?

    There are three main types of headaches:

    • Tension headaches cause relatively mild pain and occur in about 90% of the population.
    • Migraine headaches cause severe pain, nausea, vomiting, sensitivity to light and sound, and visual disturbances. Migraines affect about 12% of the population.

    Cluster Headache

    Migraine variant | Cephalgia | Vascular headaches
    • CONDITION DETAILS
    • TREATMENT OPTIONS

    Symptoms

    Sharp, burning pain on one side of the head behind one eye, eye swelling and redness, tearing, runny nose, red face

    How Common

    Fewer than 1 in 1,000 people have cluster headaches.

    Overview

    Cluster headaches occur one to three times a day and last a few minutes to several hours. These daily headache clusters occur for several weeks and then go away for months or even years. Cluster headaches are the rarest and most severe type of headache. Men tend to get them more than... read more

    What Happens with Cluster Headaches

    Risk Factors

    Family history of cluster headaches

    Diagnosed By

    Your doctor can diagnose a cluster headache by taking your medical history and doing a physical exam along with other tests as needed.
